First Test

In the second innings of the first Test, Smith scored his tenth century in the Ashes and his 25th century in Tests.

Second Test

In the first innings of the second Test his innings was paused on 80 when he was hit with a 148.7 km/h ball to the left side of his neck, under the ear, but returned to the match after passing the concussion tests and was lbw for 92. On 18 August 2019, Smith was replaced by Marnus Labuschagne on day five of the second Test, after Smith suffered a concussion on the previous day. Therefore, Labuschagne became the first player to become a concussion substitute in a Test match following a change in the International Cricket Council's (ICC) rules.

Third Test

He was ruled out of third Test with concussion.

Fourth Test

In the first innings of the fourth Test at Old Trafford Smith scored his third double-century in Test matches and third century in the series and became the first batsman to score 500 or more runs in three successive Ashes series. In the second innings he scored rapid 82 runs from 92 balls.
